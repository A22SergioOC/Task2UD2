Paris (France)

Monuments in Paris

This page talks about the main monuments of Paris and interesting places to visit in this city.

La tour Eiffel

The Eiffel Tower (or Le tour Eiffel in french) is a monument built from 1887 to 1889 by Gustave Eiffel. It was designed by Maurice Koechlin, Émile Nouguier and Stephen Sauvestre for the 1889 World's Fair. The tower is 330 metres long and it's made of wrought-iron, an iron alooy with a very low carbon content.

Musée du Louvre

The Louvre Museum (or Musée du Louvre in french) is the most visited museum in the world. The museum is housed in the Lovre Palace (built in the 13th century), and it opened on 10 August 1793. Nowadays, the museum has a lot of notable artworks, like the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.

The Arc de Triomphe

The Arc de Triomphe is one of the most famous monuments in Paris standing at the western end of the Champs-Élysées at the centre of Place Charles de Gaulle. The Arc de Triomphe honours those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars, with the names of all French victories and generals inscribed on its inner and outer surfaces. Beneath its vault lies the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier from World War I.
